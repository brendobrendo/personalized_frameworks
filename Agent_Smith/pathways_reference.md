# Toastmasters Pathways Reference

## What is Pathways?

Pathways is the Toastmasters educational program. Members choose a "path" focused on
a particular career or leadership skill set. Each path has 5 levels, each requiring
specific projects (speeches or other activities) to be completed and logged in Basecamp.

Completing a level = submitting all required projects in Basecamp and having them approved.

---

## The 11 Pathways Paths

| Path Name | Focus Area |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation Mastery | Public speaking and presentation skills |
| Leadership Development | Leading teams and organizations |
| Motivational Strategies | Inspiring and engaging audiences |
| Persuasive Influence | Negotiation and persuasion |
| Innovative Planning | Creative problem-solving and planning |
| Visionary Communication | Strategic communication |
| Dynamic Leadership | Adaptive leadership |
| Strategic Relationships | Networking and relationship building |
| Team Collaboration | Collaborative team skills |
| Effective Coaching | Mentoring and coaching |
| Engaging Humor | Using humor effectively in communication |

## Level Structure (applies to all paths)

Each path has 5 levels. All paths share the same milestone structure:

| Level | What it means | Typical # of projects |
|-------|--------------|----------------------|
| Level 1 | Mastering Fundamentals | 2–3 projects |
| Level 2 | Learning Your Style | 3–4 projects |
| Level 3 | Increasing Knowledge | 3–5 projects |
| Level 4 | Building Skills | 2–4 projects |
| Level 5 | Demonstrating Expertise | 2–3 projects + capstone |

Completing all 5 levels = **Completing the Path** (a major educational milestone).

---

## How members log progress

Members log completed projects in **Basecamp** (base.toastmasters.org). A speech at club
counts toward Pathways ONLY if:
1. The member selects the correct Pathways project when signing up for a speaking slot, OR
2. The member manually logs it in Basecamp after the fact

This is why speeches often happen without being logged — members forget the Basecamp step.

---

## Club educational goals (DCP — Distinguished Club Program)

The club earns credit toward Distinguished status when members reach these milestones:

| Goal | What counts |
|------|-------------|
| Level completions | Each member completing a Pathways level |
| Path completions | A member finishing all 5 levels |
| New member starts | A new member completing Level 1 within their first year |

The VPE's job is to help members reach these milestones, not just give speeches.

## Common member situations and what to say

| Situation | Framing that works |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Member enrolled but never started | "Have you had a chance to explore Basecamp yet?" |
| Member gave speeches but hasn't logged them | "Did you know you can log speeches you've already given retroactively in Basecamp?" |
| Member close to finishing a level | "You might be closer to finishing Level X than you think — worth checking Basecamp!" |
| Member doesn't know what path they're on | Offer a quick chat: "I can help you find your path in Basecamp in about 5 minutes" |
