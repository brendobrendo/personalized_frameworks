# Open-Ended Question Framework (FORM)

A practical question bank to guide natural, advisory conversations.

> **FORM = Family | Occupation | Recreation | Money**  
> Use these to **guide**, not interrogate. Ask → listen → go deeper.

---

# 🟦 F — Family (Build Trust + Context)

**Goal:** Surface life events and future financial needs.

- What’s keeping you busy outside of work these days?
- Do you have family here locally or spread out?
- Any big life changes coming up for you or your family?
- What does a typical week look like for you outside of work?
- Are there any upcoming milestones you’re planning for?

> 💡 Insight: Family = future financial events (moves, kids, weddings, caregiving).

---

# 🟩 O — Occupation (Income + Stability + Opportunity)

**Goal:** Understand income, trajectory, and referral angles.

- What kind of work are you in?
- How did you get into that?
- What’s been changing in your work lately?
- What are you working toward next in your career?
- Do you see your income or situation changing in the next couple years?
- How do you typically think about your compensation—steady, variable, growing?

> 💡 Insight: Occupation reveals cash flow, growth, and partner referral potential.

---

# 🟨 R — Recreation (Lifestyle + Spending Patterns)

**Goal:** Understand values, lifestyle, and spending behavior.

- What do you like to do when you’re not working?
- Any trips or travel coming up?
- What do you tend to spend your free time (and money) on?
- What’s something you enjoy that you wish you could do more of?
- How do you usually like to treat yourself?

> 💡 Insight: Recreation = where money actually flows.

---

# 🟥 M — Money (Where You Create Value)

**Goal:** Move from surface-level to advisory conversation.

## General Money Questions

- How do you currently think about your finances overall?
- What are you trying to accomplish financially over the next few years?
- How do you usually decide where to keep or move your money?
- What’s been working well for you financially?
- Anything you feel like you could be doing better or differently?

---

# 💰 Targeted: Clients with Large CD Positions

**Objective:** Shift from *rate-focused thinking* → *strategy-focused thinking*

---

## 1. Understand Their Intent

- What led you to put a good portion into CDs originally?
- What were you hoping those funds would do for you?
- How do you think about those funds today?

---

## 2. Introduce Friction (Gently)

- How do you feel about those funds being locked in at that rate right now?
- Do you ever think about what those funds could be doing differently?
- How important is liquidity vs. growth for that portion of your money?

---

## 3. Expand Their Perspective

- If there were ways to keep a level of safety but have more flexibility or upside, would that be worth exploring?
- How do you typically think about balancing safety and growth?
- Have you ever had a conversation about using a mix of strategies instead of just CDs?

---

## 4. Bridge to FA Conversation

- A lot of clients in your position start with CDs, and then at some point explore other options—has anyone ever walked you through what that could look like?
- Would it be helpful to see what alternatives exist, even just for comparison?

> 🎯 Goal: Create curiosity, not pressure.

---

# 🔁 How to Use This (In Real Conversations)

**Do NOT rapid-fire questions.**

Use this flow:
1. Ask one question  
2. Listen  
3. Pull a thread  
4. Go deeper  

### Example:

> What led you to CDs?  
> → Got it, so stability was the priority.  
> → How are you thinking about that money now?

---

# ⚡ Key Principle

- Surface-level questions → transactional interaction  
- Thoughtful questions → advisory relationship  

> You don’t need more questions.  
> You need to **use fewer, better**.
